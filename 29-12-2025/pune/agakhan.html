Aga Khan Palace


The **Aga Khan Palace**, located in Pune, Maharashtra, is one of the most important historical landmarks in India and holds great national significance. Built in **1892 by Sultan Muhammed Shah Aga Khan III**, the palace was constructed as an act of charity to provide employment and relief to people affected by a severe famine in the region. Spread over a vast area with beautifully maintained gardens, spacious halls, and elegant Italian-style architecture, the palace reflects simplicity, peace, and historical depth. The Aga Khan Palace is closely associated with **Mahatma Gandhi and the Indian freedom movement**. During the Quit India Movement of 1942, Mahatma Gandhi, Kasturba Gandhi, Mahadev Desai, and Sarojini Naidu were imprisoned here by the British authorities. Kasturba Gandhi passed away in the palace in 1944, and her memorial, along with Mahadev Desai’s samadhi, is located within the palace premises. These memorials make the palace a sacred place of remembrance and respect. Today, the Aga Khan Palace serves as a **national monument and museum**, preserving photographs, personal belongings, letters, and artifacts related to Mahatma Gandhi and India’s struggle for independence. The calm environment, lush greenery, and historical importance attract tourists, students, and history lovers from across the world. The palace stands not only as an architectural marvel but also as a powerful symbol of sacrifice, freedom, and national pride, making it one of Pune’s most cherished heritage sites.
